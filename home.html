How Does the Stock Market Work?

Key Takeaways

  1. Stocks represent ownership equity in the firm and give shareholders voting rights as well as a residual claim on corporate earnings in the form of capital gains and dividends.
  2. Individual and institutional investors come together on stock exchanges to buy and sell shares in a public venue.
  3. Share prices are set by supply and demand as buyers and sellers place orders.
  4. Order flow and bid-ask spreads are often maintained by specialists or market makers to ensure an orderly and fair market.
  5. Listing on exchanges may provide companies with liquidity and the ability to raise capital but it can also mean higher costs and increased regulation.
